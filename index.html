The Kiosk Adventure

A public interface

Today, we will explore how different users interact with a public interface. I chose one familiar to me: a boba tea place self-order kiosk. While it's a fairly simple interface, it can lead to a lot of potential interactions: what do new users feel? What about the experienced ones? How does the payment/confirmation system work? Today, we will explore some of these questions and how this kiosk addresses them!

Self-order kiosk at Ten One Tea House. The interface offers a touch screen to navigate the menu, choose a drink, customize it, as well as a payment method.

Questions and Interviews

I can tell you about the kiosks all I want, but it all comes down to people using it. So, let's take a field trip to Ten One and ask some users about their experience!

What were your expectations regarding the speed and user-friendliness of the order kiosk?

User 1: I was expecting to order within 2 minutes, and have a lot of metrics for user-friendliness such as drink parameters

User 2: I would want to order within 5 minutes, with a lot of choice options and pictures

User 3: I am not too worried about the speed of the order, but I don't want to feel pressure from talking to a person at the counter

How well did the interface match your expectations in terms of usability?

User 1: It met expectations, today I was buying drink for a friend and it was pretty straightforward

User 2: I wish there was a way to filter based on hot/cold or other parameters

User 3: It was good, but some prompts and pop-ups get in the way of ordering

How would you rate the clarity of the instructions provided by the kiosk interface?

User 1: Mostly good, but there is one additional unnecessary prompt that asks to add cups

User 2: The reward system is not super clear and does not show progress as you order drinks

User 3: It was pretty clear and helpful

Were there any points during your interaction where you felt uncertain about what to do next?

User 1: I don't think so - there are red arrows pointing to the next step of the order

User 2: I was confused in the beginning when I was asked my phone number, and was not sure if I should skip it or not

User 3: Some drinks have different customization featrues than others, which sometimes threw me off

What specific features or options led you to choose the kiosk for placing your order?

User 1: My main feature is metrics for the drink, since staff might not be accurate about them

User 2: It is important to me that the kiosk would show pictures of the drink I am getting

User 3: For me, it was the inherent feature of kiosk that allows you to fully control your order

How important is it for you to have customization options (e.g., modifying orders) available?

User 1: Very important since default settings drinks are too sweet for me most of the time

User 2: I never order pre-determined drinks (as in default settings), so I would say it is important for me

User 3: I would say it is quite important for me because it allows to experiment with different tastes

Did you experience any technical issues or glitches while using the kiosk?

User 1: No issues, and I liked the summary at the end

User 2: No techincal issues

User 3: Nothing that I can remember

What additional features or functionalities would you like to see in the kiosk interface?

User 1: I think the kiosk already has everything that's needed

User 2: As mentioned before, sort based on drink qualities, like hot or cold, or milk and non-milk

User 3: Maybe a better reward system, or a saved order for a profile

How could the interface be made more intuitive for first-time users?

User 1: Maybe a sign that says you can choose between kiosk or front desk, since they are pretty close

User 2: I think there should be an explaination of the rewards system and an option to skip it.

User 3: I would have additional prompts to help when the phone number gets registered for the first time

Key Observations

  • To each their own: Almost all interviewed users had a different primary focus: drink parameteres, ability to browse, or control over your order
  • Less talking: A common topic was also the chance not to talk to staff: for many it's less awkward and provides more info about their drinks
  • Power in numbers: There was a lot of enjoyment from choice, as a lot of conversations hit topics like customization, drink parameters, and rewards

Empathy maps

Based on the interviews and other observations, we can start thinking of abstract personas that would interact with the kiosk like that.

Ethan is a Brown University Computer Science student with a sweet tooth. He tries not to spend too much on Boba, but it’s hard to resist. He usually visits one to two times a week after his afternoon class. When he comes in, the interface is familiar to him: he enters the phone number to get that free drink soon, quickly navigates to his favorite drink, and selects desired sugar level, boba, and milk options. Sometimes, he is a bit upset that there is no default order to save, so the process takes the same amount of steps every time.

Alice takes good care of her diet and likes to know the details about her food and drinks. She appreciates a good drink but visits a similar place about once a month. As a first time user, she got a little perplexed by the “Enter phone number" screen, and took a second to find the cancel button. She wanted to filter the drinks based on calories or other criteria, but could not find the button for it. After getting to the payment screen, she instinctively moved to the bottom, bud didn't find the payment device.
